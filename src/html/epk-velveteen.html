"Velveteen Delusion"

This track is from Climate's 14th full-length release, Colorways. It's an intepretation of a basic early American pop song, and it's been intentionally stuck in the midst of an album with widely varied genres across tracks. The lyrics allude to a velveteen rabbit's transformation from a stuffed animal to something real, juxtaposed against the piquant mid-life ennui of being beaten down by children and general adult responsibilities... the likes of which will not let up until the sweet release of death.

"Velveteen Delusion" started as a little riff on baritone ukulele that Nathan came up with, then Grant took it and developed the full instrumentation. Grant says, "I initially was trying to rewrite 'I Think We’re Alone Now' by Tommy James and the Shondells. Didn’t really end up that way though." Nathan wrote the lyrics based on Grant's vocal melody. This one was sung by Grant on main vocals and Nathan added in the chorus parts.
